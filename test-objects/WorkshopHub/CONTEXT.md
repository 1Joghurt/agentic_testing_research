The application to be tested is the workshop planning tool "WorkshopHub". WorkshopHub is a platform for users who want to find, book, or organize workshops. It combines event listings, participant registrations, and capacity management within a single application.

Users can search, filter, and compare workshops based on various properties. Each workshop clearly indicates its availability status, such as available, fully booked, or cancelled. Viewing complete workshop details and performing personal actions, such as booking or organizing a workshop, requires a user account.

Registered participants can sign up for a workshop with a desired number of attendees and may optionally provide a phone number or note. A registration must not exceed the remaining workshop capacity. Duplicate active registrations and self-registration for one's own workshop are not permitted. Existing registrations can be modified or cancelled. Cancelled registrations are retained for traceability but cannot be edited further.

Any registered user can also act as an organizer and create workshops by providing a description, category, date, location, capacity, and instructor. Organizers can update details of their own workshops, such as the location or instructor, or cancel them entirely. The application does not provide a separate administrator view; instead, each user manages their own workshops while acting as a regular participant for workshops created by others. Workshops with past dates or invalid capacities cannot be created. Cancelled workshops remain visible but are no longer bookable.

Users may additionally submit ratings with stars and comments to provide feedback to organizers, who can view this feedback in their workshop management view.

The application is fully responsive. For example, depending on the screen size, workshops are displayed either as a tile grid or as a list view.
